A Safer London empowered by Data

Journal Article, Geoconnexion Magazine 19(1): 43-45

February, 2021 Yijing Li

Following the COVID-19 outbreak, we have seen large reductions on the general volume of crimes and across many crime types (i.e., acquisitive crime, knife crime) in London. The most significant drop over 1st lockdown (from March to May 2020) had occurred on Theft and Robbery with on average over 50% decrease year-on-year, especially in April, and in parallel of the crime decreases on Violence against The Person (dropped about 30% in April), and Possession of Weapons (dropped over 30% in March), which were considered to be highly related to and driven by the dramatic cliff jump of mobility (Fig.1). However, some other crimes (i.e., Domestic Abuse and Anti-Social Behaviour) saw dramatic increases over the period comparing to 2019, for example, Drug Offences had increased over 50% in May comparing to 2019.
